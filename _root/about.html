Henry Addo - @eyedol

Senior software developer at Ushahidi
Love being a software developer and try to be good at it.

A Little About Me

I am a software developer from Accra, Ghana living in Sendai, Japan. I have a strong passion for mobile development, web development and open technologies. I have been in this industry for close to 7 years now which has gained me so much experience. I have a proven track record of successful projects. I am capable of working independently and I do this well with a distributed team.

I am currently a senior software developer at Ushahidi a non-profit technology company that focuses on building tools for data collection, visualization and interactive mapping.

I have interest in meeting people and I love to connect people with the right resources and the support they need. I'm always ready to make an introduction.

Personal

I am a geek and a husband. I have practised the art of juggling between being a geek and a husband.

This year, My wife and I moved from Ghana to live in a bike-friendly city of Sendai, Japan. I cycle to work most of the time and I mostly communute through the city by bicycle. I only use the mass transit when it's necessary.

Passion

I have a keen interest in being a good photographer. This year, I faced reality and invested in a decent camera to pursue my passion.

I love open technologies and I have been instrumental in the evangelism and advocacy of the use of Free and Open Source Software in Ghana. I enjoy riding motorcycle. In fact I love anything on two wheels.

Work

At Ushahidi, I lead the development efforts of Android apps for Ushahidi's web based tools and contributes to major parts of the core Ushahidi web platform.

In 2012, I founded FindReels.com A comprehensive movie guide for Ghanaian cinemas.

In 2007, I was awarded TEDGlobal fellowship which granted me free pass to attend the prestigious TED conference in Arusha, Tanzania.

I have worked as a software developer with several IT companies Suuch Solutions, txtGhana, IDZ Ghana Limited. I also worked as a Java instructor at IPMC Ghana, an IT training institution.
